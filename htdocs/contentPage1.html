SUMMARY OF THE SOGIE EQUALITY BILL


Discriminatory Acts based on SOGIE

Please note that the way this was sectioned in this website was based on the degree of sanction for commiting these discriminatory acts.

First Section

  • Promoting and encouraging SOGIE-based stigma and/or inciting violence or abuse to someone based on their SOGIE
  • Punishing a student or a trainee more harshly than customary punishments because of their SOGIE. This includes discrimination of someone due to the SOGIE of their parent or legal guardian.
  • Refusing to acknowledge or to accredit of any organization or group of people based on their SOGIE
  • Revoking the license, permit, or any other legal documents necessary to exercise a profession or business based on their SOGIE
  • Denying a person’s use of facilities or establishments open to the public based on their SOGIE

Second Section

  • Making SOGIE a part of the criteria for employment in both private sectors and public services
  • Refusing admission or expulsion of a student based on their SOGIE
  • Denying a person’s access to health services open to the general public, and giving someone inferior services would be considered as denial of access
  • Subjecting or forcing someone to undertake diagnosis to determine or alter their SOGIE without their consent or approval. This is not the case if the person involved is a minor which involves other legal action for the diagnosis’s approval
  • Based on their SOGIE, physically and verbally harassing someone or subjecting them to unnecessary degrading investigatory activities by anyone, including members of law enforcement agencies

Third Section

  • Subjecting someone to unlawful acts based on their SOGIE that might infringe their exercise of their rights and freedom

Administrative Sanctions regarding the Act

The penalty would depend on the degree of the unlawful act committed. The refusal of a government official to investigate or act upon a complaint about a violation of this act without valid reasoning would be sanctioned as well.

Sanction for the First Section

Upon conviction, they would be:
  • Penalized by a charge of not less than P100,000 but not more than P250,000 or;
  • Not less than a year but no more than 6 years of imprisonment.

Sanction for the Second Section

Upon conviction, they would be:
  • Penalized by a charge of not less than P250,000 but not more than P500,000 or;
  • Not less than six years but no more than 12 years of imprisonment.

Sanction for the Third Section

  • Upon conviction, they would be penalized with the same sanction as the unlawful act they done.

Promoting for the inclusion of SOGIE Rights


  • Concerns with regards to the Act would be addressed by the Women and Children’s Desk available in a Police station. Those that would be handling these desks must undergo training on handling the complaint with gender sensitivity and awareness. Complainants that are minors must be accompanied by their parents in addressing the complaint.
  • Different government agencies would be more inclusive to the needs of the victims of SOGIE discrimination.
  • Different protection programs would be initiated and conducted to promote a non-discriminatory environment for SOGIE, and would encourage other sectors to participate in these efforts.
  • To overlook the implementation of this Act, the Anti-Discrimination Oversight Committee or (ADOC) has been created. This organization would monitor if public institutions abide by the provision and prohibition of the Act. After three years, they would create an audit and submit this report to the Congress, the Office of the President, and the Supreme Court before ceasing to exist after six years of the organization.
